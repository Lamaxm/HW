French Onion Soup

We have been trying French onion soup in restaurants for years and my family and friends agree none can compare to my recipe for taste and simplicity of preparation.

INGREDIENTS

- 1/2 cup unsalted butter
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 4 cups chopped onions
- 4 cans of beef broth (10.5 ounces)
- 2 tablespoons dry sherry (optional)
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme Salt and pepper to taste
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 4 slices bread French
- 4 slices provolone cheese
- 2 slices swiss cheese, diced
- 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese

    Directions

  1. Melt butter and olive oil in an 8-quart saucepan over medium heat. Add onions, stirring constantly, until soft and translucent. Do not fry the onions.
  2. Add meat broth, sherry and thyme. Season with salt and pepper and simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat the oven grill.
  4. Place soup in oven-safe serving bowls and place one slice of bread on top of each (bread may be broken into pieces if you prefer). Layer each slice of bread with provolone, half a Swiss slice, and 1 tablespoon of Parmesan cheese. The utensils are placed on a cookie sheet and roasted in the preheated oven until the cheese bubbles and is slightly browned.
